Under By-law 4 (Art. 3.1.1), approval authority for goods and services follows two separate tracks depending on whether the purchase originates from an academic or administrative department. Both tracks converge at the Director General level. The same thresholds apply to construction contracts. All amounts are before applicable taxes and must be within approved budget.

Vanier applies the LCOP tendering thresholds with an additional internal policy requiring a public tender process at 80% of the legal threshold. The current public tender threshold of $139,000 was set by the Secrétariat du Conseil du Trésor and will next be updated on January 1, 2028 per WTO inflation calculations.

Service contracts under the LGCE (Loi sur la gestion et le contrôle des effectifs) have a separate approval path from general procurement. This ensures the College does not bypass workforce controls through service contract issuance. These rules apply in addition to the general approval authority under By-law 4.

< $25K
Director of Service
The Director of the requesting service may approve any service contract valued below $25,000.
≥ $25K
Director General
The Director General must approve any service contract valued at $25,000 or above, as required by the LGCE.
By-law 9, s. 6.03: All service contracts must be authorized by the Chief Executive Officer (or delegate) prior to being awarded.

Following the May 2025 amendments to By-law 9, the previous 10% cap on contract amendments has been removed and replaced with tiered authority limits. Once a contract exceeds the original approval limit through amendments, it cannot be re-delegated to a lower authority.

Important: Once a contract amendment pushes the total value past the original approver's authority, it cannot be re-delegated downward. The higher authority must approve all subsequent amendments.

Determining whether to hire through a short-term employment contract (processed by HR/Payroll) or a service contract (processed by Purchasing) depends on the nature of the engagement. This distinction is critical because service contracts fall under LGCE oversight and procurement rules.
